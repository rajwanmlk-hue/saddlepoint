NEOM
A $500 billion smart mega-city on Saudi Arabia's northwestern coast — the most ambitious urban development project in human history

NEOM is a $500 billion smart city project spanning 26,500 km² in the Tabuk region of northwestern Saudi Arabia, announced by Crown Prince HRH Mohammed bin Salman in October 2017. The project is owned 100% by the Public Investment Fund (PIF) and governed by the NEOM Company. It encompasses four distinct sub-projects, each representing a different model of future urban living — from a zero-carbon linear city to an industrial port-city and a mountain ski resort.

NEOM is powered entirely by renewable energy (solar and wind) and governed by a special regulatory framework separate from Saudi Arabia's standard legal system. Artificial intelligence governs city operations, mobility, and infrastructure management. THE LINE is already under construction with tens of thousands of workers on-site; Sindalah island resort is the first segment targeting completion in 2024–2025.

  • THE LINE: A 170km linear city, 500m tall, 200m wide — housing 9 million residents by 2045 with no roads, no cars, and 20-minute end-to-end transit. Construction underway.
  • Oxagon: The world's largest floating industrial complex, located at the mouth of the Gulf of Aqaba — positioning NEOM as a global maritime and industrial hub.
  • Sindalah: A luxury island yacht and tourism resort in the Red Sea, accessible from THE LINE — targeting high-net-worth tourism from 2025–2026. Partially open.
  • Trojena: A mountain destination featuring the region's first outdoor ski slope, extreme sports facilities, a freshwater lake, and year-round temperate climate at altitude.

Red Sea Global (RSG) is the developer behind two of Saudi Arabia's largest tourism projects: The Red Sea and Amaala. The Red Sea project — spanning a pristine archipelago of 90+ uninhabited islands off the northwest Saudi coast — is the flagship, with first luxury resorts opened to guests in 2023. It is positioned as the world's most ambitious regenerative tourism destination, designed from scratch to have net positive environmental and social impact.

The project targets 50 resorts, 8,000 hotel keys, residential communities, a golf course, and an international airport (Red Sea International Airport — already operational) by 2030. All energy is renewable, single-use plastics are banned, and 75% of the destination is protected conservation area. RSG also develops Amaala — an ultra-luxury destination on the Triple Bay site offering wellness, arts, and mega-yacht hospitality. Amaala targets ultra-HNWI visitors.

  • The Red Sea (RSG flagship): 90+ islands, 50 resorts, 8,000+ keys, luxury residential community. First resorts open: Six Senses Southern Dunes (2023), Nujuma (2023).
  • Amaala: Ultra-luxury wellness and arts destination at Triple Bay on the Red Sea coast — targeting ultra-high-net-worth international guests. Phase 1 under construction.
  • Red Sea International Airport: Operational since 2024 — providing critical infrastructure to support tourism inflow and resort supply chains.
  • Residential component: Beachfront and island residential properties within the development — some available to qualifying investors through RSG's residential programme.

Saudi Arabia's capital of entertainment, sports, and arts — 45km west of Riyadh, spanning 334 km² of dramatic terrain

Qiddiya Investment Company (QIC) — majority owned by PIF — is developing Qiddiya as Saudi Arabia's premier entertainment, sports, and leisure destination. Located 45km west of Riyadh on dramatically rugged terrain, the 334 km² development spans six distinct themed zones and will include residential communities for 100,000+ residents alongside entertainment, sports, education, and hospitality uses.

Phase 1 construction is underway, with the Formula E street circuit operational, Six Flags Qiddiya theme park (the world's largest Six Flags park) expected to open in 2025, and the Qiddiya Stadium (20,000 capacity) under construction. The project is strategically positioned to serve Riyadh's 7.5 million metropolitan population — creating a catchment area of 25+ million within the broader Saudi population — and to attract international visitors.

  • Six Flags Qiddiya: World's largest Six Flags theme park · first-of-its-kind roller coasters and attractions. Opening: 2025–2026.
  • Qiddiya Stadium: 20,000-seat multi-purpose arena for concerts, e-sports, and events · under construction.
  • Formula E Circuit: First street circuit in Saudi Arabia operational in Qiddiya precinct — hosting Formula E races.
  • Water & Aqua parks: Themed water parks and family entertainment zones integrated into natural terrain.
  • Residential Communities: 100,000+ planned residents with mixed-income housing, serviced apartments, and villa communities embedded within the entertainment zones.
  • Speed Peak: A motorsport and adventure zone on dramatic cliff-edge terrain — one of the world's most extreme entertainment environments.

Diriyah Gate
The $20 billion restoration of Diriyah — birthplace of the Saudi state and UNESCO World Heritage Site — into a world-class cultural and heritage destination

Diriyah Gate Development Authority (DGDA) is the entity responsible for developing and managing the Diriyah historical area — the At-Turaif district designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2010. Located just 12km northwest of Riyadh's city centre, Diriyah is the birthplace of the first Saudi state (1744) and home to over 600 Najdi-style mud-brick structures of historic and architectural significance.

The $20 billion development restores and activates historic Diriyah while adding new luxury hotels, restaurants, museums, retail, a polo club, art galleries, and residential communities — all built in traditional Najdi architectural style. Diriyah is already open to visitors, with the Bujairi Terrace dining and cultural district operational since 2022 attracting substantial visitor numbers. Multiple luxury hotels are under construction including Bab Samhan (a luxury Heritage Collection resort) and several internationally-branded properties.

  • At-Turaif District: UNESCO World Heritage Site — restored 18th-century Najdi mud-brick palace complex. Open to visitors.
  • Bujairi Terrace: Premium dining and cultural destination adjacent to At-Turaif — operational since 2022. Multiple international restaurant brands.
  • Luxury Hotel Pipeline: 38+ hotels planned within Diriyah, including Bab Samhan (heritage luxury resort), Four Seasons, and other international brands.
  • Diriyah Season: Annual cultural and entertainment festival in Diriyah attracting hundreds of thousands of visitors — anchoring year-round demand.
  • Residential Communities: Traditional Najdi-style villa and apartment communities for sale within the development perimeter.
  • Diriyah Arts Dakira: New arts and culture museum district celebrating Saudi Arabia's rich cultural heritage and contemporary arts.
