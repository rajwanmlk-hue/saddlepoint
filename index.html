The Investment Case

Six Reasons to
Invest in Saudi Arabia

$3.3T Total Investment Pipeline
Unprecedented Capital Deployment
Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 mobilises an estimated $3.3 trillion in total public and private investment across infrastructure, tourism, tech, and real estate — creating demand that significantly exceeds current supply in every major city.
0% Capital Gains Tax
World's Most Competitive Tax Environment
No personal income tax. No capital gains tax on property. Transaction costs of just 2.5%. Saudi Arabia offers foreign investors exceptional after-tax returns unavailable in most Western markets.
100% Foreign Ownership Rights
Full Freehold Title Available
Saudi Arabia's investment zone reforms allow foreign nationals to hold full freehold title in approved areas, with no nationality restrictions, inheritance rights intact, and long-term residency eligibility linked to ownership.
35M Population
Young, Growing Population
With 70% of Saudis under 35 and a rapidly growing urban middle class, residential and hospitality demand is structurally rising. Vision 2030 targets 100M+ annual visitors.
3.75 SAR/USD Fixed Rate
Dollar-Pegged Currency Stability
The Saudi Riyal has been pegged to the US Dollar since 1986. Investors face zero currency volatility risk — a rare advantage for international capital in emerging markets.
24h Title Transfer
World-Class Digital Infrastructure
Saudi Arabia's fully digital deed registration system enables title transfer within 24–48 hours. Transparent, efficient, supported in English — among the fastest systems globally.

Vision 2030

The Transformation
Blueprint

Vision 2030 is Saudi Arabia's national transformation plan — the most ambitious economic diversification programme in the Arab world. Every pillar creates direct real estate demand.

01 · Economic Diversification
Non-Oil GDP to 50%
Reducing oil dependence drives massive investment in finance, tourism, manufacturing, and entertainment — all requiring commercial and mixed-use real estate at scale.
Target: SAR 1.7T non-oil GDP by 2030
02 · Tourism & Entertainment
100 Million Visitors Annually
Prior to September 2019, Saudi Arabia issued no tourist visas to general visitors. Now targeting 100M annual visitors, hospitality demand across the Kingdom is undergoing structural transformation.
150+ new tourism destinations planned
03 · Urban Development
New Cities & Districts
NEOM, Red Sea Project, Qiddiya, and Diriyah Gate represent $1 trillion+ in entirely new urban environments requiring integrated real estate ecosystems from scratch.
$1T+ in mega-project real estate
04 · Population Growth
Urbanisation & Housing Demand
Vision 2030 targets 70% homeownership rates and is attracting skilled expatriate workers. Both drivers create sustained residential demand across all price points.
1.5M new homes needed by 2030
