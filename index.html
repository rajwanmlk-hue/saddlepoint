Vision 2030

The Transformation
Blueprint

Vision 2030 is Saudi Arabia's national transformation plan — the most ambitious economic diversification programme in the Arab world. Every pillar creates direct real estate demand.

01 · Economic Diversification
Non-Oil GDP to 50%
Reducing oil dependence drives massive investment in finance, tourism, manufacturing, and entertainment — all requiring commercial and mixed-use real estate at scale.
Target: SAR 1.7T non-oil GDP by 2030
02 · Tourism & Entertainment
100 Million Visitors Annually
From virtually zero tourist visas in 2018 to a target of 100M annual visitors, hospitality demand across the Kingdom is undergoing structural transformation.
150+ new tourism destinations planned

New Cities & Districts

$1T+ in mega-project real estate
04 · Population Growth
Urbanisation & Housing Demand
Vision 2030 targets 70% homeownership rates and is attracting skilled expatriate workers. Both drivers create sustained residential demand across all price points.
1.5M new homes needed by 2030

Yes. Since 2020, Saudi Arabia allows 100% foreign ownership of property in approved zones with no nationality restrictions. Investors benefit from full freehold title, inheritance rights, and long-term residency eligibility linked to property ownership.

The minimum threshold varies by property type and location. Residential entry points start from approximately SAR 250,000, while commercial and development opportunities typically begin at SAR 1 million. Our advisors can identify opportunities that match your specific budget.

Vision 2030 is the primary driver of Saudi Arabia's real estate appreciation. The $3.3 trillion investment pipeline, mega-project development, and population growth targets create sustained demand pressure that historically translates to 12–42% capital appreciation across key markets.

Saudi Arabia offers one of the world's most competitive tax environments for property investors. There is zero personal income tax, zero capital gains tax on property, and transaction costs of just 2.5%. A 15% VAT applies to commercial transactions, though exemptions are available in certain structures.

Saudi Arabia's electronic deed registration system enables title transfer within 24–48 hours in most cases. The process is fully digital, transparent, and available with English language support. Saddlepoint manages the entire registration process on behalf of our clients.

Yes. Saudi Arabia's Premium Residency program offers long-term residency to foreign property investors meeting specific thresholds. This provides visa-free status within the Kingdom, family inclusion, and access to government services.

Property transactions are denominated in Saudi Riyal (SAR), which is pegged to the US Dollar at a fixed rate of 3.75. International investors can transact in USD, GBP, EUR, and other major currencies through approved banking channels, with full remittance rights for rental income and capital returns.
