Key Insights

What the Data
Is Telling Us

Capital Appreciation
+42%
Riyadh prime residential has appreciated over 42% between 2020 and 2025, driven by Vision 2030 infrastructure investment, Metro network completion, and population growth. The appreciation trajectory remains structurally supported through 2030.
Transaction Volume
+45% YoY
Real estate transaction volumes in Riyadh increased 45% year-over-year in 2024, according to REGA data. International capital inflows grew faster than domestic — institutional investors from the US, UK, and Asia Pacific are driving commercial sector demand.
Supply Gap
1.5M Homes
Vision 2030's homeownership target of 70% requires an estimated 1.5 million new residential units by 2030. Current supply delivery rates are tracking below this need — creating a structural supply gap that underpins residential pricing across all market segments.
Commercial Real Estate
85%+ Occupancy
Grade A office occupancy in KAFD (King Abdullah Financial District) exceeds 85%, with major multinationals establishing regional HQ in Riyadh ahead of Saudi Arabia's 2024 requirement for regional HQ presence as a prerequisite for government contracts.
Hospitality Sector
100M Visitors
Saudi Arabia welcomed 100 million visitors in 2023, meeting the Vision 2030 target seven years early. The government has now raised the target to 150 million annually by 2030 — representing a massive pipeline demand for hotel keys, serviced apartments, and hospitality real estate.
Rental Market
60–120% Premium
Premium furnished short-term rentals in prime Riyadh districts command 60–120% above long-term equivalents. Corporate demand from Vision 2030 project teams, international executives, and relocating expats drives near-permanent occupancy in KAFD, North Riyadh, and Al Olaya.

Market Questions

Data & Market
Questions

Our market intelligence is compiled from official Saudi government sources including: REGA (Real Estate General Authority) — the primary real estate regulator's price indices and transaction data; Ministry of Justice property registration records; MISA (Ministry of Investment) FDI data; ZATCA tax authority transaction data. We supplement this with reports from JLL, Knight Frank, Savills, and CBRE who cover the Saudi market. Data is updated quarterly. Where specific data points are estimates or ranges, we clearly note the source and methodology.

The rental yield figures (e.g. 7.2% for Riyadh prime residential) represent gross average yields based on REGA rental index data and market-observed transaction prices for Q4 2024 / Q1 2025. Gross yield = annual rent / purchase price. Net yield (after management fees of 5–10% and maintenance allowances) would typically be 1–2 percentage points lower. Prime commercial assets in KAFD can exceed 9–10% gross. Individual property yields vary significantly by location, condition, tenant quality, and lease terms. Saddlepoint provides property-specific yield analysis during the advisory process.

No market is without risk of correction. The structural case for Saudi Arabia's real estate market is unusually well-supported by factors that insulate it from typical correction triggers: (1) Government capital deployment is committed and budgeted — PIF's $925B+ AUM directly funds mega-project demand. (2) Supply is structurally short — the 1.5M home deficit means organic demand exceeds supply. (3) There is no sub-prime lending risk — Murabaha mortgage LTVs are capped and underwriting is conservative. (4) The SAR/USD peg eliminates currency devaluation risk. The primary risks are oil price shock reducing government spending capacity and mega-project delivery delays — both are legitimate risk factors we monitor actively for clients.

Both markets offer 0% capital gains tax and 100% foreign ownership in designated zones. Saudi Arabia's advantages: (1) Higher average rental yields (7.2% vs. Dubai's 5.8%); (2) Stronger 5-year capital appreciation (42% vs. 28%); (3) Larger domestic demand base — Saudi's 35.8M population vs. UAE's 9.9M; (4) $3.3T government investment pipeline vs. Dubai's smaller but also substantial pipeline; (5) Dollar peg equally stable (UAE peg since 1997, KSA since 1986). Dubai's advantage: more mature, internationally liquid market with a longer track record and greater resale velocity. We advise clients considering both markets and can structure a dual-market portfolio approach.
